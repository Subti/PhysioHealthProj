Tennis Elbow

Understanding and Managing Tennis Elbow

Tennis elbow, also known as lateral epicondylitis, is a condition that causes pain around the outside of the elbow. It is often caused by overuse of the forearm muscles and tendons, including repetitive twisting of the wrist and arm extensions. This condition is common among people who play racquet sports, hence the name, but do not be fooled because it can and mostly occurs in anyone who repetitively uses their wrist or clenches their fingers.

Causes of Tennis Elbow

  • Overuse: The primary cause of tennis elbow is the overuse of the muscles and tendons in the forearm. This can be due to repetitive wrist and arm motions.
  • Sports Activities: Tennis elbow is common in racquet sports players, but any repetitive gripping activities, especially if they involve twisting the wrist, can lead to this condition.
  • Non-sporting Activities: Certain professions or activities that involve repetitive arm motion, such as painting, typing, or using a screwdriver, can also cause tennis elbow.

    • (Harvard Health Publishing, Mayo Clinic)

Effects on the Body

  • Pain and Tenderness: Tennis elbow can cause pain and tenderness on the outside of the elbow, which might radiate into the forearm and wrist.
  • Grip Weakness: You might experience weakness in the grip of the affected arm.
  • Limited Movement: If the condition worsens, you might have limited movement in the elbow.

    • (Harvard Health Publishing, Mayo Clinic)

Prevention and Management

Preventing neck and back pain largely involves lifestyle changes. Here are some key strategies:

  • Regular Breaks: If your work or activity involves repetitive motion of the wrist or arm, take regular breaks to prevent overuse and strain.

  • Correct Technique: Whether you are involved in a sports like Tennis or in a job/hobby that requires repetitive wrist movements, ensure that your techniques and wrist movements are correct to avoid unnecessary strain on your forearm muscles.

Conclusion

Despite its more commonly known name, Tennis Elbow can affect people across a wide variety of different occupations and sports, and the discomfort it can cause could heavily impact your daily activities. The good news is that there are several effective treatments for tennis elbow, including physical therapy, NSAIDs, braces, and sometimes surgery, if conservative methods fail to provide relief.

Remember, prevention is the best cure - employing correct techniques and postures, using ergonomic tools, and ensuring regular rest and muscle conditioning can significantly reduce the risk of developing this condition.
